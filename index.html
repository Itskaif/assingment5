Donate for Flood at Noakhali, Bangladesh

Noakhali, a coastal district in southeastern Bangladesh, is prone to flooding due to its geographical location and low-lying terrain. The district is crisscrossed by several rivers, such as the Meghna, which often overflow during the monsoon season, exacerbated by heavy rainfall and tidal surges from the Bay of Bengal. Flooding in Noakhali results in widespread inundation of farmlands, homes, and infrastructure, leading to displacement of residents, loss of crops, and disruption of livelihoods. The situation is often worsened by poor drainage systems and rising sea levels linked to climate change. During severe floods, emergency services struggle to provide aid, and many areas become isolated, further complicating relief efforts. Despite these challenges, the people of Noakhali show resilience, rebuilding their lives in the aftermath of frequent floods.

Donate for Flood Relief in Feni, Bangladesh

The Feni district, located in southeastern Bangladesh, is frequently affected by flooding due to its low-lying topography and monsoon rains. Heavy downpours, often exacerbated by the overflow of local rivers such as the Feni River, lead to widespread inundation, impacting both urban and rural areas. The flooding disrupts daily life, damaging homes, infrastructure, and agricultural lands, which are vital to the local economy. Displaced families face significant challenges, including loss of livelihoods and access to clean water and sanitation. Community resilience is tested as locals come together to assist one another in recovery efforts. Despite the recurrent nature of these floods, the people of Feni demonstrate remarkable strength and solidarity in rebuilding their lives and infrastructure in the aftermath.
